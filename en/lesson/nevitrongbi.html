Dermal Melanocytic Nevus

Definition / General

A dermal melanocytic nevus is a benign proliferation of nevus cells (a type of melanocyte) that are located within the dermis layer of the skin. These nevi are common skin lesions that vary in size, shape, and color but are typically brown, tan, or flesh-colored. They are composed of clusters of melanocytes and can occur anywhere on the body.

Essential Features

  • Benign proliferation of melanocytes within the dermis
  • Typically presents as a small, well-demarcated, pigmented lesion on the skin
  • Can vary in color from tan to dark brown; some may be skin-colored
  • Generally stable in size and appearance over time but may evolve slowly.

Terminology

  • Also known as intradermal nevus when predominantly located in the dermis
  • May be referred to as a mole in common language

Epidemiology

  • Highly prevalent in the general population, with the number of nevi increasing through adolescence and gradually declining with age.
  • Occurs in individuals of all races and ethnicities, with some variation in prevalence and appearance

Pathophysiology

  • Arises from the proliferation of melanocytes, which are pigment-producing cells originating from the neural crest.
  • The exact cause of this proliferation is not fully understood but is believed to involve genetic factors and sun exposure

Clinical Features

  • Typically appears as a small, round to oval, pigmented lesion with a smooth surface
  • Can be found anywhere on the body but are most common on areas exposed to the sun
  • Rarely causes symptoms but can occasionally become irritated or inflamed

Diagnosis

  • Primarily based on clinical examination.
  • Dermoscopy can be useful in assessing the lesion's pattern and structure to differentiate it from malignant melanoma
  • Biopsy and histological examination may be necessary if the lesion is atypical or shows signs of change.

Laboratory

  • Not applicable for diagnosis; however, histopathological examination of a biopsy specimen is the gold standard for confirming the diagnosis

Radiology Description

  • Typically, radiological imaging is not required for the diagnosis of dermal melanocytic nevi

Gross Description

  • Grossly, dermal melanocytic nevi are well-circumscribed, pigmented lesions that may have a smooth, verrucous, or papillomatous surface.

Microscopic (Histologic) Description

  • Characterized by nests of nevus cells within the dermis, often extending into the deep dermis
  • Nevus cells are melanocytes that do not have dendritic processes and are usually larger than the surrounding dermal cells
  • These cells may be arranged in clusters (nests) or as single units within the dermis
